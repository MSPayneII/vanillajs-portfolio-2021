TL ; DR

Our team partnered with four Michigan city and township Clerk's Offices ( Bay City, Northville Township, Oxford Township, and Ferndale ) to roll out an automated email confirmation system to notify voters, in those areas, of their absentee applications and ballots status. We are now in the asssessment phase of the project, collecting voter feedback regarding their experience with the system.

The project originated during my summer internship as a Citizen Interaction Design Summer Fellow in May 2020. As a fellow, I worked in interdisciplinary teams to provide solutions to Michigan municipalities and organizations' information issues.

Problem Statement

In 2020, due to the passage of the statewide ballot proposal 18-3, all eligible and registered voters in Michigan may now request an absent voter ballot without providing a reason. With existing limitations on resources and the increase in completed applications, Michigan's absentee voting system is facing increasing constraints, especially in the realm of transparency.

The Objective

To expand implementation of our automated email notification system, reduce the time City Clerk offices spend answering AV ballot questions and help Clerks implement a simple means to promote voter confidence via ballot transparency, we will need to achieve the following:

  • Demonstrate the impact of last summerâ€™s implementations in Oxford Township, Northville Township, Ferndale and Bay City
  • Implement a sustainable plan and process for the notification system, including staffing and technology
  • Give clerks the ability to give voters status information regarding their AV ballots, regardless of their technical capabilities.
  • Motivate the State of Michigan to take on the larger project and move it forward so clerks state-wide can easily access it
